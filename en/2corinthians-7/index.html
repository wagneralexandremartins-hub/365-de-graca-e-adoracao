2 Corinthians — Chapter 7

Godly Sorrow

"For godly sorrow produces a repentance that leads to salvation without regret."

— 2 Cor 7:10

2 Corinthians 7 recounts Paul's relief upon receiving good news from Corinth through Titus, and teaches the crucial distinction between godly sorrow and worldly sorrow.

💙 Godly Sorrow vs. Worldly Sorrow (7:8-13)

2 Cor 7:10-11
"For godly sorrow produces a repentance that leads to salvation without regret, whereas worldly sorrow produces death. For behold, what earnestness this godly sorrow has produced in you!"
The fundamental distinction: godly sorrow (kata theon lype) leads to repentance (metanoia) and salvation; worldly sorrow (tou kosmou lype) leads to death. Godly sorrow is productive — it produces earnestness, defense, indignation, fear, longing, zeal, and punishment.
