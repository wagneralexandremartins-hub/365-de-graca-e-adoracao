📖 Analysis of Matthew 15

Matthew 15:1
"Context and introduction to chapter 15"
Matthew 15 is set within the larger context of the Gospel as a testimony to the Davidic Messiah. Matthew, writing for a Jewish audience, constantly connects the events of Jesus’ life with Old Testament prophecies, demonstrating that Jesus is the fulfillment of all Israel’s hopes. In this chapter, we see another aspect of Jesus’ ministry that reveals His identity as the Son of God and Savior of the world. Matthew’s narrative is carefully structured into five major discourses (chs. 5–7, 10, 13, 18, 24–25), interspersed with narrative sections that demonstrate Jesus’ authority in word and deed.
Matthew 15 — Central Theology
"The main theological themes of this chapter"
Among the central theological themes of this chapter are: the sovereignty of Jesus over all creation, the call to radical discipleship, the fulfillment of messianic prophecies, and the expansion of the Kingdom of Heaven. Matthew presents Jesus as the new Moses who gives a new Law, the new David who establishes an eternal kingdom, and the Suffering Servant of Isaiah who bears the sins of the world. Each episode narrated by Matthew is selected and arranged to build this multifaceted portrait of the Messiah. The reader is invited not only to know Jesus intellectually but to follow Him as a disciple—which implies learning, obeying, and witnessing.
